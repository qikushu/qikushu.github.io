      title: Research
      text: |
        Rice is one of the world's three major cereal crops. Due to its small genome size, established transformation systems, and extensive genetic and breeding knowledge, rice is an important model for biotechnology-based breeding and plant genome research.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: "1. Gene Mapping and Cloning in Rice"
      text: |
        Our laboratory has identified the 12 linkage groups corresponding to the basic chromosome number of rice and established marker gene sets. Using RFLP markers and Recombinant Inbred (RI) lines, we have constructed high-density linkage maps. Map-based cloning of genes such as the Daikoku-type dwarf gene (*d1*) has been successfully accomplished.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: "2. Genetic and Breeding Studies of Various Traits"
      text: |
        We conduct genetic and breeding research on domestication-related traits (shattering, awning, grain size), reproductive isolation traits (hybrid sterility, hybrid breakdown), environmental adaptability (heading date), and insect resistance (green rice leafhopper, whitebacked planthopper). QTL analysis using RI lines and chromosome segment substitution lines is a key approach.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: "3. Evolution and Differentiation of Cultivated Rice"
      text: |
        Using RFLP analysis of cultivated and wild species, we explore the phylogenetic relationships within the genus *Oryza*, particularly among A-genome species distributed across tropical Asia.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: "4. Cytogenetic Studies of the Genus Oryza"
      text: |
        We have developed enzymatic maceration techniques for karyotype analysis of rice somatic chromosomes and have established various aneuploid and chromosomal structural variant lines. Monosomic alien addition lines carrying single wild species chromosomes are used to analyze wild species characteristics and explore introgression of useful genes.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: "5. Development and Utilization of Experimental Line Populations"
      text: |
        This is our most unique research activity, providing essential genetic materials for all other research themes. We develop RI lines, chromosome segment substitution lines (CSSLs), and introgression lines from AA-genome wild species including *O. glaberrima*, *O. glumaepatula*, *O. meridionalis*, *O. rufipogon*, and *O. nivara*. Over 2,000 marker gene lines, structural variant lines, aneuploid lines, and mutant lines are maintained.
